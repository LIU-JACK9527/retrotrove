## What Are Retro Games?

Retro games, also known as classic games, are video games that were released in the late 20th century, typically from the 1970s through the 1990s. These games are characterized by their simple graphics, straightforward gameplay mechanics, and memorable soundtracks. They’re the games that defined entire generations of gamers and laid the foundation for the modern gaming industry.

Some of the most iconic retro game systems include the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), Super Nintendo (SNES), Sega Genesis / Mega Drive, and classic arcade machines. These systems introduced us to legendary characters like Mario, Sonic, Link, and Pac-Man, creating universes that continue to captivate gamers today.

What makes retro games so special is their timeless appeal. Unlike many modern games that rely on cutting-edge graphics and complex mechanics, retro games focus on pure gameplay. They’re easy to pick up and play, yet often offer surprising depth and challenge.
